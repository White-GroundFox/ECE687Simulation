Done — approach_robot_node.py is in your Windows repo and compiles cleanly. How it works:

Goal pose is recomputed every cycle from robot 6's live mocap pose: stand STANDOFF_DISTANCE away along APPROACH_BEARING_DEG in robot 6's own frame (0° = in front of its gripper), heading turned 180° so robot 3 faces it — gripper to gripper, like your photo. If you later want to approach from the side or behind, just change the bearing (90 = its left, 180 = behind).
Three phases: NAVIGATE uses the course's approximate linearization — control the point p at l = 0.15 m ahead, then [v, ω] = L⁻¹(l)Rᵀ(θ)ṗ (the Eq. 2–3 from the project PDF); ALIGN rotates in place to the exact final heading (the offset-point method alone doesn't guarantee it); HOLD publishes zeros — and if robot 6 moves, it automatically re-navigates.
Velocities are clamped (0.5 m/s, 1.5 rad/s) and the Ctrl+C safety stop works (same SignalHandlerOptions fix as your other nodes).
One flag on your numbers: you asked for a 2 m standoff, but the sim field is only 4×4 m and in your photo robot 3 ends up ~0.6 m from robot 6. With STANDOFF_DISTANCE = 2.0, the goal point will often land outside the walls depending on where robot 6 faces. It's the first constant in the file — try 0.6 if you want the photo's behavior.
